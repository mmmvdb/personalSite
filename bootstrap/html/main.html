What is this?

This is an experiment in Bootstrap doubling as a personal profile. This is a double whammy, two birds with one stone, playground of sorts. This is a way to tell you who I am, while at the same time I get to play with a new tool I taught myself, Bootstrap 4!

One of the many things I enjoy about life is learning about everything. New discoveries in physics. Fresh recipes in the kitchen. And of course new ways to tackle tough problems in software development. There is nothing that excites me more than encountering new things. Unless it's meeting people with the same passions that I do.

So when it came time to sit down to introduce myself to the wide world I wanted to learn a new way to do it. I read through documentation most would find dry but I found new and exciting. I spent time working with the new concepts to hone them into actual knowledge.

And finally I get to use that knowledge to craft a responsive webpage and show you my personality.

Caveat

There is a bit of a stigma around bootstrap that sites that use it all look the same. This seems mainly due to the fact that people just copy and paste the first template they find on google, with no modifications or customizations. I've tried to avoid that by not using a template and allowing myself to code the layout of the page myself. I've only borrowed example components within the page from the documentation.

I've heard it said that every want to be webdeveloper also codes up a page like this for their portfolios when in truth it is nothing special. There may be truth to that, because it even at this early stage of my learning about it, it seems very easy to quickly get content up with no fuss. A bit of pragmatism is good here, and knowing the tools available to you and what they are good for is important. Bootstrap is good at getting responsive content up with no complications and would be a good tool for prototyping or setting up layout on something that needed to be more involved or complex later.

It has done it's task for me here, and on top of learning it has introduced me to some other things that I can now learn about, such as SCSS (Sass), LESS, and webdeveloper tools such as Koloa that can do some CSS compiling and autoprefixing of projects. This just gives me more to learn so I consider this experiment a success.
